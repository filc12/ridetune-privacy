Privacy Policy — RideTune

Last updated: 3 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the RideTune mobile application ("RideTune", "the app", "we") handles information. RideTune is a motorcycle suspension setup reference tool. We have designed the app to be private by default.

Summary

RideTune does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. Everything you enter stays on your device. There are no accounts, no servers operated by us that store your data, and no third-party analytics or advertising trackers.

Information stored on your device

To work, the app saves the following information locally on your device only (using your device's standard app storage):

  • Your rider name (optional, used to personalise the app)
  • Your rider weight and load details (used to calculate suspension setups)
  • Saved suspension setups and rider profiles you create
  • Your language preference and app settings

This information never leaves your device. We cannot see it, and it is not uploaded anywhere. If you uninstall the app, this information is deleted.

Information we do NOT collect

  • We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or location.
  • We do not use analytics, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs.
  • We do not track your activity or build a profile of you.
  • We do not share or sell any information to third parties.

Internet access

RideTune works offline for its core function. The app does not require an account or login and does not send your personal data over the internet.

Children

RideTune is intended for motorcycle riders and a general audience. It does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children.

Safety notice

RideTune provides suspension setting starting points based on manufacturer specifications and general references. These values are guidance only and are not a substitute for the official owner's manual, a sag measurement, or professional advice. Incorrectly adjusted suspension can affect motorcycle stability and safety. You use the app and any settings it suggests at your own risk, and you are responsible for the final setup of your motorcycle.

Changes to this policy
